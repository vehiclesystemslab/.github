# VEHICLE Systems Lab

**An independent technology research laboratory developing auditable architectures, simulations, and decision-support systems for complex environments.**

Founded on the **Borda Milan Pyramid** — a coherent, observable, and empirically testable architectural framework for studying complex systems across science, technology, society, nature, defense, and global decision-making.

---

## What we build

VEHICLE Systems Lab does not build isolated software. We build **structured systems** that document their logic, preserve traceability, allow human review, and expand into specialized applications through a coherent architectural core.

Every project inherits from **MADRE** — the living structural core of the laboratory — and operates as an independent structural child capable of growing into its own domain while maintaining coherence, auditability, and ethical boundaries.

---

## MADRE — The Core Architecture

> *MADRE is not a product. It is the mother structure from which all VEHICLE systems are born.*

MADRE organizes knowledge through **layers, nodes, attractors, contractors, and informational mitosis**. Its purpose is to allow each project to preserve traceability, internal coherence, and capacity for expansion — while remaining explainable to humans at every stage.

**Status:** Active training and research consolidation. No public demo yet — by design. MADRE's demonstration is built after the training structure, not before.

**Projected commercial value:** USD 100,000,000 at full operational capacity.

## The Borda Milan Pyramid

The Borda Milan Pyramid is the central research framework of VEHICLE Systems Lab. It provides a structured method for:

- organizing knowledge across structural layers
- mapping relationships between nodes, attractors, and contractors
- detecting systemic tensions at different scales
- supporting coherent analysis across scientific, institutional, social, and planetary domains

**Central research question:**
> *Can the Borda Milan Pyramid serve as a coherent, observable, auditable, and empirically testable framework for understanding and supporting decisions in complex systems across different areas of science and society?*
